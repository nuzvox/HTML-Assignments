Who we are and What we do

We are U.A.C Professionals, also known as U.A.C, an American computer manufacturer and retailer. We specializes in a wide range of business and gaming-oriented computer hardware, ranging from low cost/low performance to high cost, custom built high performance, we are particularly known for our custom-built machines which are produced via our website orders and additional for accepting requests for parts that we do not advertise on our website.

U.A.C Professionals were founded and incorporated on December 10, 1993 in Yonkers, New York.

In 2003 our company was listed as one of the fastest growing privately owned business in the Yonkers borough by the Yonkers Business Journal.

From 2011-2020 we have been consistently ranked within the top 150 largest privately owned companies headquartered in Yonkers by the Yonkers Business Journal.

We produces and sells PCs used either private businesses or in computer gaming. Our products feature third-party components prepared into the complete ready-to break packages; and we offers the option for our users to choose their custom components for building PC.

Everything went well. System shipped on the expected date (a Friday) and UPS actually delivered a day before the 3 business days were up (on Tuesday instead of Wednesday). Packing was excellent and everything arrived in excellent condition. Willis Leung was my rep and did a good job of communicating progress in building the system. One part was "almost" late, but must have arrived the day before the system needed to ship (he notified me that there might be a problem and asked if I wanted to substitute for that part). I told him a substitute was OK, but the original brand item was in the system, so it arrived in time for the build. The build was well done. Fairly neat wire routing, everything hooked up and working. My only (very minor) issue was noise from the system case fans. This was caused by using the included case fan distribution setup, which seems like a solution in search of a problem. Using the fan distro board forces all the fans (CPU and case) to run at the same speed as each other. And it seems like all of them were running pretty fast and not changing based on need. So I moved all the fans back to plug into the motherboard where I could set their speed in the bios, based on temperature. Now my system is whisper quiet. Inside of case is at 35C, CPU running at ~ 55C. If I'd known there was a fans distro board in the case, I would have ask Willis to leave it out of the loop......but I can't fault U.A.C for using it.
— Miranda (Wilqua)
